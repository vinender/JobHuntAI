When Copilot arrived, I did not adopt it because it was trending. I adopted it because I immediately felt what it unlocked — not replacing thought, but removing the friction between an idea and its execution. What used to take a day of research and debugging now takes an hour. The instincts are still mine. The speed is not.
Where others saw friction in using AI tools, I felt the opposite — like having every piece of knowledge one prompt away. That feeling never faded. It still drives how eagerly I reach for these tools and how deeply I push them.
I treat prompting as a craft. When I encounter an unfamiliar stack, my first move is to build something real with it — the Laravel + SQL project, the AI video clipper, the PDF toolkit — none of these were in my existing skill set before I built them.
I am interested in how LLMs work at a deeper level, not just what they produce. That curiosity is what keeps the output quality consistently high.
